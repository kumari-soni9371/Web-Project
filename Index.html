Recipe1: Litti Chokha

Ingredients:-

For Litti (Stuffed Wheat Flour Balls):

  • Whole wheat flour-2 cups
  • Sattu (roasted gram flour)-1 cup
  • Caraway seeds (ajwain)-1 tsp
  • Cumin seeds-1 tsp
  • Ajwain (carom seeds)-1 tsp
  • Fresh ginger-1-inch piece (finely chopped or grated)
  • Green chilies-1-2 (finely chopped)
  • Mustard oil-1 tbsp (for binding the dough)
  • Salt-to taste
  • Water-for kneading the dough
  • Ghee or oil-for basting (while baking or frying)

For Chokha (Mashed Vegetables):

  • Eggplant (baingan)-1 large
  • Tomatoes-2 medium
  • Potatoes-2 medium
  • Green chilies-2 (finely chopped)
  • Garlic-4-5 cloves (finely chopped)
  • Ginger-1-inch piece (finely chopped)
  • Mustard oil-1 tbsp
  • Coriander leaves-for garnish
  • Lemon juice-1 tsp

Instructions for Litti:-

Prepare the Dough:

  • In a large bowl, add the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  • Add mustard oil, and slowly pour in water to knead the dough. The dough should be smooth and soft, like chapati dough.
  • Cover the dough and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

Prepare the Filling:

  • In a separate bowl, mix sattu, ajwain, cumin seeds, chopped ginger, green chilies, and salt.
  • Add a bit of water and mustard oil to make the filling slightly moist but not too runny.
  • You can also add a little garam masala or chopped coriander leaves for extra flavor.

Shape the Litti:

  • After the dough has rested, divide it into small equal-sized balls.
  • Flatten each ball into a disc using your hands or a rolling pin.
  • Place a spoonful of the sattu filling in the center of each disc.
  • Carefully fold the edges of the dough around the filling and seal it, forming a round ball.

Cooking the Litti:

  • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F). Place the littis on a baking tray and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  • Alternatively, bake them on low heat in a traditional clay oven (battis).
  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan and fry the littis until they are crispy and golden brown.

Basting:

  • Once baked or fried, brush the littis with ghee or oil to give them a rich flavor.

Instructions for Chokha:-

Roast the Vegetables:

  • Roast the eggplant (baingan) over an open flame, or bake it in an oven at 200°C (400°F) until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  • Roast the tomatoes as well, either on the open flame or in the oven, until the skin is charred and the tomatoes become soft.
  • Boil the potatoes until they are tender.

Prepare the Chokha:

  • Once the vegetables are roasted, peel off the skin of the eggplant and tomatoes. Mash them in a bowl.
  • Peel and mash the boiled potatoes as well.
  • In a pan, heat mustard oil and add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  • Add the mashed vegetables into the pan. Add salt and lemon juice, and mix everything well.

Final Garnish:

  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Speciality:-

Litti Chokha is a traditional dish from Bihar, India, which consists of litti (wheat flour dough balls stuffed with roasted gram flour) and chokha (a mashed vegetable mix, usually consisting of roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and potatoes).It is a hearty, flavorful meal that is often served with chutney or pickles.

Recipe2: Tomato Soup

Ingredients:-

  • 6-7 medium-sized ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small carrot (optional), chopped (for sweetness and texture)
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp dried oregano or basil (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream or milk (optional, for creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:-

Prepare the Tomatoes:

  • Wash the tomatoes thoroughly and chop them into small pieces.
  • Optionally, if you want a smoother soup, you can blanch the tomatoes in hot water for a minute, then peel off the skin and chop them.
  • This step helps make the soup smoother, but it's not necessary if you prefer a rustic texture.

Sautéing the Vegetables:

  • In a large pot, heat butter or olive oil over medium heat.
  • Add the chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes until soft and translucent.
  • Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  • f you're using carrot, add it at this stage and sauté for 3-4 minutes to soften it slightly.

Cooking the Tomatoes:

  • Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes break down and release their juices.

Add Broth and Seasoning:

  • Add vegetable broth or water to the pot along with salt, black pepper, and dried oregano or basil if using.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil and then reduce the heat. Let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, until the tomatoes and other vegetables are well-cooked and softened.

Blending the Soup:

  • Use an immersion blender directly in the pot to blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can transfer the mixture to a blender in batches and blend until smooth.
  • If you want a silky texture, you can strain the soup through a fine mesh sieve to remove any remaining pulp or seeds.

Finishing Touches:

  • Once blended, return the soup to the pot and bring it to a simmer again for a few minutes.
  • Add sugar (if desired) to balance the acidity of the tomatoes and stir in fresh cream or milk to make the soup creamy and smooth.

Serve:

  • Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or herbs if necessary.
  • Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves or a swirl of cream, if desired.

Speciality:-

Tomato Soup is a comforting, versatile, and classic dish enjoyed worldwide, especially during colder months.Tomato soup is packed with vitamins and minerals, especially Vitamin C and Vitamin A, due to the high content of tomatoes.It's a comfort food, offering a warm and soothing texture.
